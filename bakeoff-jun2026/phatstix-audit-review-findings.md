# Phatstix Audit — Fable Review Findings (11 Jun 2026)

## Verdict in one paragraph

The audit's strategic diagnosis survives adversarial verification — margin geometry is the real problem, AOV-first is the right fix, "restructure, don't scale" on Meta is correct, and the headline data (Meta spend £6,118 at campaign scope, GSC positions 5–9, GA4 revenue within 3% of the claimed £5,775) was accurate at the 31 May pull. But three classes of defect must be fixed before this deliverable governs another week of Thom's time. **First, the arithmetic breaks at exactly the points the plan steers by**: the target operating model (Scenario 3 "+£400/mo") computes to roughly break-even on the page's own formula; the £29.70 affordable CAC is priced at full price while every ad pushes the £43 discounted 2-pack (true CM2 ≈ £25.70); and the Meta CPA target band (£25–30) sits at or above contribution per order at the target AOV — a fully on-target week loses money on every paid order while the scorecard shows green. **Second, the plan assumes away the supply chain and the bank**: "restock is unlimited & fast via Here2Grow" is factually false (~300 units/month, Black Friday 2025 stocked out, single-source China tubes), no page models stock or cash, and ~£10k of committed/looming H2 outflows sit against ~£7.7k of free cash after VAT. **Third, it was stale within ten days of delivery**: Meta was cut to £30/day on 3 June (the −£1,200/mo baseline is now ≈ £0 to −£400/mo and "hold £1.5–2k/mo" silently became a 2× increase order), email campaigns have been dormant since 3 January (the audit describes a live "~1/month cadence"), and the headline "Meta over-reports ~2.5×" is a broken-UTM bucketing artifact — the defensible factor is ~1.4×. Add one compliance stop-ship: "7× bigger" is unsubstantiated as printed and "lips, cheeks and lids" exceeds the assessed CPSR category. None of this reverses the strategy; nearly all of it changes the numbers, the sequencing, and what week one actually contains.

## Critical findings

### C1 · Supply is assumed infinite — "Restock is unlimited & fast via Here2Grow" is factually false, and no page in 17 workstreams models stock
**Pages:** 30 · Relaunch Playbook, 90-Day Roadmap, 01 · Economics, Weekly Review Prompt. **Type:** infeasible / data error.
**What:** The Playbook asserts unconstrained supply three times — "Restock is unlimited & fast via Here2Grow after", "unlimited H2G stock" (Stage 2), and "you can restock freely" (Stage 1, where it is load-bearing for the waitlist-over-preorder call). Ground truth: H2G fills ~300 units/month at the current catch-up cadence, Black Friday 2025 was under-stocked — empirically falsifying "unlimited & fast" in exactly the window the plan calls "the biggest revenue window" — and tubes are single-sourced from Elva (China; 5,000 inbound from 13 Jun cover the relaunch run, so the binding constraint is H2G fill capacity, with tube risk on the reorder).
**Evidence:** Steady state is fine (Scenario 3 ≈ 135 units/mo vs 300 capacity), but the stacked Q4 peak is not: Dec 2025 did 72 orders while supply-constrained (Economics even cites that censored December as the demand seasonality peak without flagging it), and a successful Q4 at ~3× run-rate is ~450–650 units across Nov–Dec vs a max 600 of production with zero left for relaunch follow-through or wholesale. The Weekly Review lists "a stockout" as a red flag but pulls no inventory data. A 2-week December stockout at planned velocity forfeits ~£2,400–3,700 of contribution — roughly the entire 90-day operating improvement promised.
**Edit:** Add an "Inventory & Capacity" section to the Playbook and a stock gate to Roadmap Phase 3: (1) correct all three "unlimited/restock freely" claims to "~300 units/month at current catch-up — confirm true max run-rate and lead time with H2G", and revisit waitlist-vs-charge-upfront-preorder in that light; (2) a monthly S&OP table Jun–Dec (opening stock, production in, demand by phase incl. relaunch + Q4 + wholesale allocation); (3) a named Black Friday buy quantity with an order-by date (~Aug/Sep at 300/mo), anchored to the BF25 understock; (4) book continuous ≥300/mo H2G runs by 20 June and lock Q4 volume + a wholesale allocation rule by 31 July; (5) add "weeks of stock cover at trailing 4-week velocity" to the Weekly Review PULL list, red flag at <6 weeks; (6) gate Q4 paid scale on weeks-of-cover, not just positive blended contribution; (7) one-line tube risk note: single-source China, set a cover-based reorder trigger and get one UK/EU backup quote before Q4.

### C3 · Affordable CAC is priced at a price you don't sell at, and the CPA target band exceeds contribution per order
**Pages:** 01 · Economics §3/§5, spine, 13 · Paid Meta, 18 · AOV, Weekly Review Prompt. **Type:** maths error (confirmed factual).
**What:** £29.70 two-balm break-even CAC assumes the full-price £48 order — but the plan's own default offer is "any 2 = 10% off + free delivery" (£43/£43.20), and Fix 2 points every ad at it. By the audit's own formula, the merchandised 2-pack CM2 = **£25.65–25.81**, so "an improved Meta CPA (£25–30 target) clears it" is false for most of the band: a £30 CPA loses ~£4.35 per 2-pack. The spine's "selling two nearly doubles your affordable CAC (£15.90 → £29.70)" is **+61%** at the live offer, not ~2×. Compounding it: at the Weekly Review's own target state (AOV £40, 1.5 units, £3.30 COGS) contribution = **£24.67/order — below the entire £25–30 green band** — so a fully on-target week is contribution-negative on every paid order, masked in the blend by organic mix. The 3-pack (£61, −15%) yields CM2 ≈ £37 and genuinely clears £30.
**Edit:** Economics §3: show both rows (£48 full-price £29.70 reference-only; merchandised £43 → ~£25.70) and rewrite the bullet: "Two-balm break-even CAC ≈ £25.70 at the live offer; Meta CPA must land ≤ ~£25 (working target ~£22) to leave margin." Spine + AOV-18 callout: "+~60%, not nearly double." Paid Meta 13: tighten the targets-table CPA to ≤£25 and set Fix 4's ASC cost cap off £25.70, not £30. Weekly Review: CPA target £20–25 (≤£20 green, £20–25 amber) plus a COMPUTE rule — "flag the CPA row RED if Meta CPA ≥ contribution per order, regardless of the static target." Optionally note the 3-pack as the ad offer if CPA can't hold under ~£25.
